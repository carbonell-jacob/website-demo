From Playtime to Pressure: My Path to Becoming Me

Biography of Jacob S. Carbonell

CIP_1101 CSAD

Childhood Years

My name is Jacob Sioson Carbonell1. I am currently a 19 year-old Filipino college student living in Parañaque2. Looking back at my childhood, I can’t seem to remember a time when I was ever sad3. My parents have always told me that when I was young, I was very hyper4. It was true, I was always the loudest in the house when I played and messed around5. I had a lot of toys gifted to me by my aunt that I would play with every day6. My favorite toy is LEGO7. I remember building random structures instead of following the guides and spending hours finding the pieces that I needed8. I didn’t have access to the internet at a young age9. They were against it because they believed it would affect my development10. We had a family computer but they put a password on it so I couldn’t use it11. The only time I remember I used a computer was when I played snail Bob online with my father12. During my elementary school days in St. John Paul II Academy, I don’t remember doing a lot of schoolworks at all13. I was a very social person, so instead of studying I spent most of the time hanging out with my friends14. This was the reason why I was put into tutoring for multiple subjects by my parents15. My favorite subject back then was Christian Living because it was the easiest and the one I was most familiar with since our family is religious16. I met two of my closest friends during this time17. Sometimes we would talk about our childhood memories in and out of school, reminiscing the fun times that we had together18. Childhood felt like I had all the freedom to do whatever I wanted19. I don’t remember crying at all, and I was thankful for that because I would remember how truly happy I was20.

Teenage Years

I was taught to always be patient and have a positive outlook on life by my parents1. They always tell me that I should always serve as a good example to my younger brother2. This was when I realized it was time to grow up3. I noticed that I started to have responsibilities, feel pressure academically, and become self-aware4. When I was around 9th to 10th grade I realized there is more to life than just playing around, I had to be serious now5. I started to take academics seriously in junior and senior high school6. I disciplined myself to do my work diligently, practiced proper time management, and conditioned my mind and body to become more responsible, all while applying the principles I learned at home7. I did not become a top student by doing all of these, in fact, I am just average, but I know that these qualities will help me in the long run8. I met new friends during this time, and through this process I learned more about myself9. In contrast to my loud and hyper childhood self, I realized I am actually a quiet and introverted person10. I also found out I like to play musical instruments such as piano and guitar, and found new hobbies such as drawing, coding, playing video games, cycling, and going to the gym11. I experimented with a lot of things to find my interests12. I tried all the clubs in school, watched different TV shows and movies genres, and played all kinds of sports such as basketball, table tennis, and badminton13. In doing this I found out I like playing basketball, watching sitcoms and anime, and cooking, so I joined the cooking club in my school14. Being a teenager, I was not exempted from puberty15. It was kind of a difficult time for me because this was the only time I felt a strong feeling of insecurity16. When people told me I looked and sounded different, I felt embarrassed and didn’t want to show myself to anyone17. I felt really confused and unsafe, but thankfully my parents helped and supported me throughout these times18. Overall, my teenage years are an integral part of my life19. This is when I started exploring different things and finding myself in the process20.

College Years

After senior high school, it was time to apply for college1. I applied to three universities, Far Eastern University Alabang, Polytechnic University of the Philippines, and University of the East Manila2. I remember studying for the entrance exams for sleepless nights, living off of coffee3. After finding out that I passed all of them, I remembered being so happy it paid off4. When I began studying in UE Manila, I was overwhelmed by how different it is to my previous school5. The culture of this university is deeper and more diverse, the campus is bigger, and there are way more students6. The learning environment in college is also different compared to what I was used to7. I realized that I am not studying to excel in my grades anymore, I study to survive8. There are times when it gets really exhausting and stressful, but I just try my best to remind myself that as long as I do my best, everything will be fine9. The overwhelming amount of work is an opportunity for me to improve my time and stress management10. I realized that I have to adjust for a while11. In my first two weeks, I found my circle of friends, and I am happy that I am still with them now12. College wouldn’t be complete without them because they serve as my support group13. To not get burned out, I try to balance my academic and personal life14. However, I still struggle sometimes because I don’t know how to do it properly15. Overall, college has been a fun ride so far16. There are times where I feel lost and overwhelmed, but I know that everything I experience here has a purpose17. It was never going to be an easy journey in the first place18. Computer science has thrown different kinds of challenges at me, and I know it will continue to do so in the next 2 years19. I just hope that by then, I will face it as a more well-rounded individual20.
